// about
Why Nowruz Club?

Nowruz marks the moment Earth's axis tilts — a reset available to everyone. We borrowed that energy for how we work together.

The problem we saw

Talented persian language professionals settling in Canada and Australia have time, skills, and motivation — but limited ways to contribute before they're fully embedded in the local economy. Skills go stale, connections are missed, and potential is left untapped.

What we're building

A skill-project marketplace. Every two weeks, projects that need technical help (charitable, community, or open) are matched with contributors who have the expertise and bandwidth. We work in the open — no secrets, no NDAs.

Why "Nowruz"?

Nowruz is defined by Earth's position — not by language, religion, or ethnicity. It's a universal reset. Our fortnightly sprint cadence mirrors that: close one cycle, reflect, open the next. The name signals renewal, not exclusion.

Who can join?

Anyone with skills to offer or an open project that needs help. We skew toward the persian language diaspora in Australia and Canada because of shared context, but the club is culturally open. If your project is in-the-open, you're welcome.

How a sprint works

Inspired by Scrum, adapted for an open volunteer community. Every two weeks is a new cycle.

current → Sprint 1 Farvardín 1405 · 2 weeks ● active
Sprint Opening
Process & tooling setup. Chose GitHub for project management, adopted Scrum-inspired flow, selected pilot projects.
Grooming
Product owner defines deliverables. Tasks clarified, scope confirmed, dependencies mapped.
Planning
Tasks estimated and assigned. Contributors pick up items matching their skills.
Mid-sprint check
Added by the team (not standard Scrum). A one-week pulse check to catch blockers early.
Review and Retrospective
Show what was built. Reflect on the process. Feed learnings into the next sprint.

// contribute
How to get involved

Three paths in. Pick whichever fits your situation.

01

Join the Telegram group

Say hello. Introduce yourself. Read through recent sprint notes to understand what's active. No commitment yet — just show up.

02

Attend a Sprint Opening (as a listener)

Sprint Openings are public. Come in silent mode, watch how projects are presented and contributors assigned. Dates are on the website. This is the fastest way to see if this is for you.

03A

Bring a project

Have a community, charitable, or open project that needs IT help? One requirement: it must be in-the-open — no confidentiality. Bring it to the next Sprint Opening and we'll find contributors.

03B

Contribute to a project

Browse open projects. If a project matches your skills — web dev, design, data, PM, anything — join the sprint. You get real work, new skills, and a network. The project gets your expertise.
